[Notes]

The switching threshold is set by the divider: VREF = VDD × R2 / (R1 + R2). With R1 = R2 and VDD = 5.00 V, VREF = 2.50 V.
Connect VIN to the non-inverting input and VREF to the inverting input, so the output switches from LOW to HIGH when VIN rises above 2.50 V.
Because U1 has an open-collector output, R3 is required to pull VOUT HIGH.
To keep the threshold within 2.50 V ±0.05 V, use a regulated 5.00 V supply and precision divider resistors.
U1 pin 7(OUT2) is NC because the second comparator output is unused; pins 5 and 6 are tied to GND so the unused comparator does not float.
