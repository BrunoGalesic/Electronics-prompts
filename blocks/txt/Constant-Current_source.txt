[Notes]
This is a two-terminal constant-current source using an LM317A adjustable regulator.
The external load connects between IOUT and GND.
The regulator maintains approximately 1.25 V across R1, so the load current is set by:
ILOAD ≈ 1.25 V / R1
With R1 = 124 Ohm:
ILOAD ≈ 1.25 / 124 = 10.08 mA
This nominal current is within the required 10.0 mA ±0.5 mA range.
Use a precision regulator grade such as LM317A and a tight-tolerance resistor for R1 to preserve the ±0.5 mA requirement.
A 0.1% resistor is recommended for best accuracy and repeatability.
Load-range check:
For RLOAD = 100 Ohm:
VLOAD = 10.08 mA × 100 Ohm ≈ 1.01 V
For RLOAD = 500 Ohm:
VLOAD = 10.08 mA × 500 Ohm ≈ 5.04 V
The LM317A current source requires compliance headroom of approximately:
VLOAD + 1.25 V + regulator dropout
With a 12.0 V supply and a maximum load voltage of about 5.04 V, there is sufficient voltage headroom across the full 100 Ohm to 500 Ohm load range.
Therefore this circuit provides approximately 10 mA through any load from 100 Ohm to 500 Ohm, provided the load is connected from IOUT to GND.
C1 is the local input bypass capacitor for regulator stability and supply decoupling.
