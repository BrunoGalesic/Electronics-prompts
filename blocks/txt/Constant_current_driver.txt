Circuit: 5.0V to 10mA LED Constant-Current Driver
Source: Designed solution

[Components]
U1 = TLVH431B, Package=SOT-23, Function=1.24V precision shunt reference controlling LED current
Q1 = MMBT3904, Package=SOT-23, Function=NPN transistor low-side current sink
R1 = 124 Ω ±1%, Package=0603, Function=Current-sense resistor setting LED current
R2 = 2.20 kΩ ±1%, Package=0603, Function=Bias resistor for U1 cathode and Q1 base drive

[Notes]
- U1, Q1, and R1 form a low-side constant-current sink. U1 regulates the SENSE node to approximately 1.24 V, so the LED current is set by R1: I_LED ≈ 1.24 V / 124 Ω = 10.0 mA.
- With a 0.5% reference and a 1% current-set resistor, the steady-state LED current is approximately 9.85 mA to 10.15 mA, which satisfies the 10 mA ±1 mA requirement.
- R2 provides cathode current for U1 and base drive for Q1.
- Net LED_K is the connection between the LED cathode and the Q1 collector.
- Net BIAS is the shared control node connecting U1 cathode, Q1 base, and R2.
- Net SENSE is the current-sense node connecting U1 REF, Q1 emitter, and R1.
- The circuit requires sufficient compliance voltage; with a 5.0 V supply it is suitable for typical indicator LEDs whose forward voltage leaves headroom for Q1 and the control loop.
