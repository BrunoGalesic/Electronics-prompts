[Notes]

This is a linear regulator solution; it is the simplest way to guarantee a stable 3.00 V output from a 5.0 V input while supplying 20 mA.
Regulator enable pin is tied high to +5V, so the circuit turns on whenever input power is present.
C1 should be placed close to U1 IN, and C2 close to U1 OUT, to maintain regulator stability.
C3 on the BYPASS pin reduces output noise and improves reference performance.
Load power is:
Pload = 3.0 V × 20 mA = 60 mW
Regulator dissipation is:
Preg = (5.0 V - 3.0 V) × 20 mA = 40 m
so no heatsink is required.
To meet the requirement 3.00 V ±0.10 V, choose a fixed 3.0 V LDO whose total output accuracy is better than ±3.3%. The LP2985-30 class of regulator is appropriate.
If the 5 V source is noisy or comes through long wiring, optionally place an additional 100 nF ceramic capacitor in parallel with C1.
