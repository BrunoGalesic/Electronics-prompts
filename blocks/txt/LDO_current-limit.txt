[Notes]

This is a discrete linear regulator: U1 senses VOUT through R2/R3 and adjusts Q1 base current to regulate the output near 3.0 V.
R2 and R3 set the nominal output voltage: VOUT ≈ 2.495 V × (1 + R2/R3) = 2.495 × (1 + 2.00k/10.0k) ≈ 2.99 V.
R4 and Q2 implement current limiting. When the drop across R4 reaches about one VBE, Q2 turns on and steals base drive from Q1, limiting output current to approximately 0.65 V / 22 ohm ≈ 29.5 mA under short-circuit conditions.
Net Q1E was inferred as the pass-transistor emitter / current-sense node connecting Q1 emitter, R4, and Q2 base.
R1 = 560 ohm provides enough current for U1 cathode bias plus Q1 base drive at 20 mA load while keeping dissipation low.
Use 1% resistors for R2 and R3, and a TL431A-grade reference (or equivalent accuracy grade) to comfortably meet the 3.00 V ±0.10 V requirement over normal part tolerances.
C1 provides output decoupling and improves loop stability; increase if the load is connected through longer wires.
