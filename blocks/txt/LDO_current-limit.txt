[Notes]

U1, U2, Q1, and the R1/R2 divider form a closed-loop linear regulator.
Output setpoint is defined by VOUT = VREF × (1 + R1 / R2) = 2.500 V × (1 + 2.00 kΩ / 10.0 kΩ) = 3.00 V.
Use 0.1% tolerance for R1, R2, and U2 to comfortably meet the 3.00 V ±0.10 V requirement over 0 mA to 20 mA load.
R4 and Q2 implement short-circuit current limiting. Approximate limit is I_LIMIT ≈ V_BE(Q2) / R4 ≈ 0.6 V / 24 Ω ≈ 25 mA; even with V_BE ≈ 0.7 V, current is about 29 mA, still below the 30 mA requirement.
At full load (20 mA), R4 drops about 0.48 V, so Q1 still has ample headroom to regulate 3.00 V from a 5.0 V input.
R6 pulls Q1 base toward its emitter to reduce pass current when U1 output is high or unpowered.
C1 is local supply bypassing for U1.
C2 is the output reservoir capacitor.
C3 provides loop compensation by rolling off the feedback path at high frequency, improving stability with the PNP pass transistor.
U2 pin 1 is marked NC because it is unused for this shunt reference package variant.
