[Notes]
- This is a non-inverting Schmitt trigger using one section of the LM393.
- R1 and R2 create a fixed reference: VREF = VDD × R2 / (R1 + R2) = 5.00 V × 10 kΩ / (10 kΩ + 10 kΩ) = 2.50 V.
- VIN is not connected directly to the comparator input; it is applied through R4 to the non-inverting summing node IN1+.
- R5 feeds back the output voltage to IN1+ and creates hysteresis.
- The switching condition is set by: VIN(th) = VREF × (1 + R4/R5) - VOUT × (R4/R5).
- With R4 = 10.0 kΩ and R5 = 49.9 kΩ, R4/R5 ≈ 0.2004.
- When VOUT is LOW (approximately 0 V), the rising threshold is: VIN(th+) ≈ 2.50 × (1 + 0.2004) = 3.001 V.
- When VOUT is HIGH (approximately 5.00 V), the falling threshold is: VIN(th-) ≈ 2.50 × (1 + 0.2004) - 5.00 × 0.2004 = 1.999 V.
- Therefore the designed thresholds are approximately 3.00 V rising and 2.00 V falling, which satisfies the ±0.1 V requirement.
- Because the LM393 output is open collector, R3 is required to pull VOUT HIGH.
- U1 pin 7(OUT2) is intentionally unconnected. U1 pins 5 and 6 are tied to GND so the unused comparator does not float.
- Use a regulated 5.00 V supply and precision resistors to preserve threshold accuracy.
