[Notes]

This is a window comparator. The resistor string R1-R2-R3 generates two reference voltages from the 5 V supply.
With R1:R2:R3 = 20k:10k:20k, the divider current is uniform and the tap voltages are approximately VREF3 = 3.0 V and VREF2 = 2.0 V.
Comparator 1 checks the lower threshold: U1A compares VIN against VREF2 with VIN on the non-inverting input and VREF2 on the inverting input, so its output is HIGH when VIN > 2.0 V.
Comparator 2 checks the upper threshold: U1B compares VREF3 against VIN with VREF3 on the non-inverting input and VIN on the inverting input, so its output is HIGH when VIN < 3.0 V.
The LM393 outputs are open-collector, so OUT1 and OUT2 are tied together on net OUT and use a single pull-up resistor R4. This forms a wired-AND function: OUT is HIGH only when both comparator outputs release the line.
Therefore OUT is HIGH only when 2.0 V < VIN < 3.0 V, and LOW otherwise.
Required operating points are satisfied: VIN = 1.5 V gives OUT LOW, VIN = 2.5 V gives OUT HIGH, and VIN = 3.5 V gives OUT LOW.
Net VREF3 was inferred as the 3.0 V divider tap connecting R1, R2, and U1(IN2+).
Net VREF2 was inferred as the 2.0 V divider tap connecting R2, R3, and U1(IN1-).
C1 is a local bypass capacitor for stable comparator supply operation.
