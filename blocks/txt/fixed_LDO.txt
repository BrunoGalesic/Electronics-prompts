[Notes]

This is a fixed-output linear regulator solution using a 3.0 V LDO / low-dropout regulator.
The regulator output is nominally 3.00 V.
A 1% output-voltage tolerance corresponds to:
VOUT = 3.00 V ± 0.03 V
or 2.97 V to 3.03 V
This is comfortably within the required 3.00 V ± 0.10 V range.
With a 5.0 V input and 3.0 V output, the regulator has 2.0 V headroom, which is sufficient to maintain regulation over the required 0 mA to 20 mA load range.
At the maximum load current of 20 mA, the minimum equivalent load resistance is:
RLOAD(min) = 3.00 V / 20 mA = 150 Ohm
The regulator power dissipation at maximum load is:
PD(max) = (5.0 V - 3.0 V) × 20 mA = 40 mW
C1 provides input decoupling for supply stability.
C2 provides the output capacitance required for regulator stability and load-transient performance.
Therefore this circuit meets the requirement that the output remain 3.00 V ± 0.10 V for any load current from 0 mA to 20 mA.
