[Notes]

VIN is the external input source: a 1 kHz sine wave, 10 mV peak, referenced to GND.
This is a single-stage common-emitter amplifier. C1 AC-couples the input into the base while R1 and R2 establish the DC bias point.
The divider R1/R2 sets the base near 0.84 V, so the emitter sits near 0.14 V and the transistor operates in forward-active region.
With R3 = 5.6 kohm, the collector DC voltage is approximately 2.65 V, giving a valid mid-supply operating point and plenty of headroom to avoid clipping.
At the quiescent current, the small-signal emitter resistance is about 61 ohm. The approximate voltage gain is therefore: Av ≈ -R3 / (R4 + re) ≈ -5600 / (330 + 61) ≈ -14.3.
For a 10 mV peak input, the output is approximately 10 mV x 14.3 ≈ 143 mV peak, which is within the required 100 mV to 150 mV peak range.
Because the output is taken directly at the collector, it remains centered around its DC operating point of about 2.65 V rather than around 0 V, so the waveform does not clip under the specified small input level.
The output is inverted relative to the input, which is normal for a common-emitter stage.
This gain estimate assumes the output is observed by a high-impedance load (for example, a scope input or load >= 100 kohm). A heavier load will reduce gain.
C2 is a local supply bypass capacitor to keep the stage stable and reduce supply-borne signal disturbances.
